In this course, you’ll build a practical foundation for working confidently with today’s leading AI tools. You’ll gain a clear understanding of how large language models (LLMs) work, where they add value, where human judgment matters, and how to use them responsibly in real workflows.

Each week, you’ll move through lesson videos, hands-on demos, and guided exercises designed to help you apply AI to the work you already do. You’ll explore ethical decision-making, experiment with tools, and design prompts and workflows that deliver reliable results.

By the end of the program, you’ll complete a final project that shows where AI can support your goals and how to carry these skills into your everyday work.

What You’ll Learn

Responsible AI Use

Learn core principles for safe and ethical AI use, including data privacy, bias, and how to avoid common risks in real-world workflows.

Productivity Workflows

Use AI to streamline tasks and support better decisions, from research and drafting to analysis and refinement.

Prompting Foundations

Build strong prompting habits using simple, repeatable frameworks for clearer and more dependable results.

How You’ll Learn

Learn at your own pace

Each week includes short, focused videos that walk you through key AI concepts—from understanding tools to applying them in real scenarios.

Join live sessions

Join six (6), 60-min live sessions with AI experts to see concepts in action and get your questions answered. See course calendar for session times and dates. 

Reflect and apply

Wrap up each week with a guided reflection activity to reinforce what you’ve learned and track your progress as you build your skills.

Your Journey Ahead

Here’s what you’ll be building, practicing, and applying along the way:

Phase 1

Foundations of AI Literacy

Learn how modern AI tools work, where they add value, and where caution is needed. Understand key concepts, strengths, limitations, and how to assess outputs.

Phase 2

Ethical & Responsible AI Use

Apply ethical frameworks to real scenarios. Practice identifying risks like bias, privacy, and over-reliance while learning to use AI thoughtfully and responsibly.

Phase 3

Applied AI Skills

Put your learning into action. Practice effective prompting, test workflows, and apply AI to real use cases as you prepare for Project Week.
